# VISION.md Reference Template

Use this as structural guidance when generating the founder's VISION.md.
Every section must be populated with real content from the interview.
Do not copy this template verbatim — adapt every section to the specific company.

---

## File Header

```markdown
# VISION.md — [Company Name]

*This is the Founder's strategic vision document. The CEO reads this every heartbeat
to guide all decisions. It is the constitution of the company — stable, authoritative,
and rarely changed. All operational documents derive from this.*

**Last updated:** [today's date]
**Author:** [Founder's name]
```

---

## Section: Mission

1-2 sentences. What the company does, who it serves, and the one thing it never is
(e.g. "we sell software, we don't operate casinos"). Should be memorable and specific
enough to guide decisions.

---

## Section: 12-Month Goal

One concrete, measurable target derived from Q2 and Q4. Include the revenue figure
and a statement about the founder's involvement level (from Q1).

---

## Section: 3-Year Vision

Where the company is heading at a larger scale. Extrapolate from the 12-month goal —
more revenue, wider recognition, a more mature product/platform, still self-sustaining.

---

## Section: Revenue Model

Bold the core value proposition (e.g. "One-time license. No revenue share.").
If pricing tiers exist, present them in a markdown table with tier name, price,
and key inclusions. Note the live source of truth if pricing is defined on a website.
State explicitly what requires founder approval (discounts, custom pricing, bundles).

---

## Section: Target Customer

**Primary:** One sentence defining the lead persona — who they are, what they care
about, why they're the primary focus for messaging.

**Secondary:** Bullet list of other valid customer types. Note they're in scope
but messaging leads with the primary.

---

## Section: Growth Strategy

State the budget first (e.g. "€0 — organic only"). Then list channels in priority
order with a brief note on approach for each. Include any restrictions or principles
(e.g. "no black-hat SEO, no spam, no paid placement").

---

## Section: Sales Model

One bold line describing the model (e.g. "Self-serve with live chat and email support").
Then 2-3 sentences on how it works in practice — what does the selling, where customers
buy, what support exists, any exceptions (e.g. enterprise onboarding call).

---

## Section: Product Direction

State whether the product is sellable now. Then list the roadmap in priority order
(e.g. Now / Short-term / Medium-term / Long-term). Note what the CEO cannot do
without founder involvement.

---

## Section: Organisational Structure

Include an ASCII org chart showing the hierarchy. Always include:
- Founder at top
- CEO below Founder
- Chief of Staff below CEO
- All department heads below CoS

Add a paragraph defining the CEO's role (strategic, not operational) and the CoS's
role (operational backbone). Reference the CoS agent profile file location.

Example chart:
```
Founder ([Name])
└── CEO Agent
    └── Chief of Staff (CoS) Agent
        ├── Head of Marketing
        │   └── Head of SEO
        ├── Head of Sales
        ├── [other agents...]
```

---

## Section: Operating Philosophy

This section is always included regardless of what the founder said. Explain
Research → Plan → Execute as the non-negotiable operating model. Cover:

1. **Gate 1 — Research**: What it means, who does it, what output it produces
2. **Gate 2 — Plan**: What a plan must contain, who approves it, who holds it
3. **Gate 3 — Execute**: How execution is scheduled (by the CoS into the master calendar)

Include one concrete example of what goes wrong without this model, relevant to
the founder's industry (e.g. for a SaaS blog: publishing too many posts per day
and hurting SEO; for a community strategy: posting without understanding platform
norms and getting banned).

Note who owns each gate: CoS owns the pipeline, CEO enforces the standard, department
heads execute within approved plans.

---

## Section: CEO Mandate

Three subsections:

**Autonomous — no founder approval needed**
List everything from Q11. Always append:
- "Commission and delegate research tasks to appropriate agents"
- "Hire and onboard the Chief of Staff agent"
- All items should note they operate "within an approved plan" where relevant

**Requires Founder Approval — always, no exceptions**
List everything from Q12. Phrase as absolute — "always, no exceptions."

**Reporting Cadence**
Describe frequency and format from Q13. If the founder references an existing
heartbeat spec, name it as the format source. Always note that reports are never
skipped, and that each report includes a pipeline snapshot (research / planning /
execution / blockers).

---

## Section: Guiding Principles

3-6 principles derived from Q16 and the overall interview. Each principle should:
- Have a bold title
- Have 1-3 sentences explaining what it means in practice
- Feel specific to this company — not generic startup wisdom

Always include the Research → Plan → Execute principle as one of them, even if
the founder didn't explicitly name it. Reconcile it with "done beats perfect" if
both apply.

---

## Section: What Success Looks Like

Three time horizons (3 months, 12 months, 3 years) drawn from Q15, Q2/Q4, and the
3-year vision. Keep it concrete and measurable.
